Definition 8.2.13. Projection onto a Subspace of \(\R^n\).

Suppose \(W\) is a subspace of \(\R^n\) with orthogonal basis \(\{\mathbf{f}_{1}, \mathbf{f}_{2}, \dots, \mathbf{f}_{m}\}\text{.}\) If \(\mathbf{x}\) is in \(\R^n\text{,}\) the vector
\begin{equation} \mathbf{w}=\mbox{proj}_W(\mathbf{x}) = \mbox{proj}_{\mathbf{f}_1}\mathbf{x} + \mbox{proj}_{\mathbf{f}_2}\mathbf{x} + \dots + \mbox{proj}_{\mathbf{f}_m}\mathbf{x}\tag{8.2.3} \end{equation}
is called the orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{x}\) onto \(W\text{.}\)
