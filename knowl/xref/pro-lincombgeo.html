Algorithm 2.2.10.

Given two non-collinear vectors \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{v}\) in \(\R^2\text{,}\) and a vector \(\mathbf{w}\text{,}\) we can express \(\mathbf{w}\) as a linear combination of \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{v}\) as follows:
  1. Draw lines \(L_{\mathbf{u}}\) and \(L_{\mathbf{v}}\) determined by \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{v}\text{,}\) respectively.
  2. Through the head of vector \(\mathbf{w}\text{,}\) draw lines \(P_{\mathbf{u}}\) and \(P_{\mathbf{v}}\text{,}\) parallel to \(L_{\mathbf{u}}\) and \(L_{\mathbf{v}}\text{,}\) respectively.
  3. Let \(A\) be the point of intersection of \(P_{\mathbf{u}}\) and \(L_{\mathbf{v}}\text{.}\)
  4. Let \(B\) be the point of intersection of \(P_{\mathbf{v}}\) and \(L_{\mathbf{u}}\text{.}\)
  5. Let \(O\) denote the origin. Then \(\overrightarrow{OA}=k_1\mathbf{v}\) and \(\overrightarrow{OB}=k_2\mathbf{u}\) for some scalars \(k_1\) and \(k_2\text{.}\)
  6. We have \(\mathbf{w}=k_2\mathbf{u}+k_1\mathbf{v}\text{.}\)
