Exercise 17.

We call matrices \(A\) and \(B\) orthogonally similar (and write \(A \stackrel{\circ}{\sim} B\)) if \(B = P^{T}AP\) for an orthogonal matrix \(P\text{.}\)
  1. Show that \(A \stackrel{\circ}{\sim} A\) for all \(A\text{;}\) \(A \stackrel{\circ}{\sim} B \Rightarrow B \stackrel{\circ}{\sim} A\text{;}\) and \(A \stackrel{\circ}{\sim} B\) and \(B \stackrel{\circ}{\sim} C \Rightarrow A \stackrel{\circ}{\sim} C\text{.}\) (This means that ``orthogonally similar" is an equivalence relation.)
  2. Show that the following are equivalent for any pair of symmetric matrices \(A\) and \(B\text{:}\)
    1. \(A\) and \(B\) are similar.
    2. \(A\) and \(B\) are orthogonally similar.
    3. \(A\) and \(B\) have the same eigenvalues.
