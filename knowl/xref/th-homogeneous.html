Theorem 2.4.7.

Suppose \(\mathbf{x}_p\) is a particular solution of \(A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b}\text{.}\)
  1. If \(\mathbf{x}_h\) is a solution of the associated homogeneous system, then \(\mathbf{x}_p+\mathbf{x}_h\) is a solution of \(A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b}\text{.}\)
  2. If \(\mathbf{x}_1\) is a solution of \(A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b}\text{,}\) then there exists a solution of the associated homogeneous system, \(\mathbf{x}_h\text{,}\) such that \(\mathbf{x}_1=\mathbf{x}_p+\mathbf{x}_h\text{.}\)

Proof.

We will prove part Item 2. The proof of part Item 1 is left to the reader.
[Proof of Item 2]: Let \(\mathbf{x}_h=\mathbf{x}_1-\mathbf{x}_p\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} A\mathbf{x}_h=A(\mathbf{x}_1-\mathbf{x}_p)=A\mathbf{x}_1-A\mathbf{x}_p=\mathbf{b}-\mathbf{b}=\mathbf{0} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}_1=\mathbf{x}_p+\mathbf{x}_h \end{equation*}
