Theorem 7.1.3.

The following properties hold for vectors \(\mathbf{u}\text{,}\) \(\mathbf{v}\) and \(\mathbf{w}\) in \(\R^n\) and scalar \(k\) in \(\R\text{.}\)
  1. \(\displaystyle \mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}=\mathbf{v}\cdot\mathbf{u}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle (\mathbf{u}+\mathbf{v})\cdot \mathbf{w}=\mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{w}+\mathbf{v}\cdot \mathbf{w}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \mathbf{u}\cdot (\mathbf{v}+\mathbf{w})=\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}+\mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{w}\)
  4. \(\displaystyle (k\mathbf{u})\cdot \mathbf{v}=k(\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v})=\mathbf{u}\cdot (k\mathbf{v})\)
  5. \(\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{u}\geq 0\text{,}\) and \(\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{u}=0\) if and only if \(\mathbf{u}={\bf 0}\text{.}\)
  6. \(\displaystyle \norm{\mathbf{u}}^2=\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{u}\)
  7. \(\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0 \) and \(\mathbf{u} \neq \mathbf{v} \) if and only if \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{v}\) are orthogonal.
