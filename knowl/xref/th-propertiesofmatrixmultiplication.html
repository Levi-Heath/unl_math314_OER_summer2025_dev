Theorem 3.1.20. Properties of Matrix Multiplication.
The following hold for matrices \(A,B,\) and \(C\) and for scalar \(c\text{,}\)
  1. Left Distributive Property
    \begin{equation*} A\left( B+C\right) =AB +AC \end{equation*}
  2. Right Distributive Property:
    \begin{equation*} \left( B+C\right) A=BA+CA \end{equation*}
  3. Associativity:
    \begin{equation*} A\left( BC\right) =\left( AB\right) C \end{equation*}
  4. Matrix multiplication behaves well with scalar multiplication:
    \begin{equation*} c(AB)=(cA)B=A(cB) \end{equation*}
  5. Multiplicative Identity:
    \begin{equation*} AI=IA=A \end{equation*}
