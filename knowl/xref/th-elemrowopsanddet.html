Theorem 5.2.6.

Let \(A=\begin{bmatrix}a_{ij}\end{bmatrix}\) be an \(n\times n\) matrix.
  1. If \(B\) is obtained from \(A\) by interchanging two different rows, then
    \begin{equation*} \det{B}=-\det{A}. \end{equation*}
  2. If \(B\) is obtained from \(A\) by multiplying one of the rows of \(A\) by a non-zero constant \(k\text{.}\) Then
    \begin{equation*} \det{B}=k\det{A}. \end{equation*}
  3. If \(B\) is obtained from \(A\) by adding a multiple of one row of \(A\) to another row, then
    \begin{equation*} \det{B}=\det{A}. \end{equation*}
