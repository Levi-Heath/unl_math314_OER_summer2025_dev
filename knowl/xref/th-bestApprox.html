Theorem 8.6.12.

Let \(A\) be an \(m\times n\) matrix, let \(\mathbf{b}\) be a column vector in \(\R^m\text{.}\) Consider the matrix equation
\begin{equation*} A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b}. \end{equation*}
  1. Any solution \(\mathbf{z}\) to the normal equations
    \begin{equation*} \left(A^TA\right)\mathbf{z}=A^T\mathbf{b} \end{equation*}
    is a best approximation to a solution to \(A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b}\) in the sense that \(\norm{\mathbf{b}-A\mathbf{z}}\) is minimized.
  2. If the columns of \(A\) are linearly independent, then \(A^TA\) is invertible and \(\mathbf{z}\) is given uniquely by
    \begin{equation*} \mathbf{z}=\left(A^TA\right)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b}. \end{equation*}
